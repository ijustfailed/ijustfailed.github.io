What is Failure?

Failure is not negative. It's often an opportunity! You're here at this webpage to learn more about it: follow through until the end to change how you approach failures.

Failure?

Failure is not always negative-- and the sooner you realize this the better prepared you will be for the future. But don't take it from us! Take it from these well-known individuals:

Thomas Edison

“I have not failed. I’ve just found 10,000 ways that won’t work.”

J.K. Rowling

“It is impossible to live without failing at something, unless you live so cautiously that you might as well not have lived at all- in which case, you fail by default”

Milton Hershey

Milton Hershey’s first two chocolate businesses went bankrupt, but his perseverance led him to be extremely successful with the third.

Winston Churchill

“Success is not final, failure is not fatal; it is the courage to continue that counts.”

Why Fail?

Failure forces you to: leave your comfort zone, experience new things, learn more in life, and grow. You'd rather be a heartbeat than a straight line, not only is there more variation, but there is excitement to look forward to and greater heights to reach.

Distance yourself, temporarily

Take time after a failure to process, do not act on instinct or impulse
Talk with friends, family, or other people you are close with in life
Get your mind off failure with things like exercise, music, drawing, or even television (acting on emotion may make things worse)

So now you know that failure happens everywhere. How do different disciplines deal with it?

Science

When failure occurs: Desired or expected results do not occur, failed experiments, wrong hypothesis
How it's handled: New strategy and hypotheses are created, scientific method is analyzed and critiqued
How it's handled: New discoveries and solutions are needed

Athletics

When failure occurs: Always winners and losers in sports, falling short of desired goals or results
How it's handled: Very emotional, need time to recover and rebound, frustrating but teammates and coaches can help with support and guidance
How it's handled: Helps build grit and character, failure can be used as motivation for the future

Politics

When failure occurs: Wrong decisions, uncontrollable events not handled properly, poor leadership, not being honest
How it's handled: Quality apology can help restore trust, use information for better decisions and actions in the future
How it's handled: If handled properly, can lead to greater trust in the government, helps drive social and economic change, gets rid of things from the past that are no longer beneficial (laws, rules, guidelines)

Economics/Business

When failure occurs: Lack of money or success, many startup companies are victims to failure, new products or businesses do not gain support
How it's handled: Many support systems and backups in place by banks, government, etc., worst case will have to close or shutdown, can be saved by a change in structure/product
How it's handled: Leads to new innovation, teaches lessons for the future, allows new ideas to be introduced to the public and the market

Art and Music

When failure occurs: Extremely subjective, attributed to the public’s interest in certain products or pieces, does one ever actually “fail” in art?
How it's handled: Very personal because art is created by individuals, try new pieces and methods for your work
How it's handled: Creates new ideas and more understanding about certain topics or subjects
